Space / Interior

A Store That Classifies Daily Life

MUJI's official store classification across clothing, care, daily goods, furniture and storage, and food works like a small city. Before buying, the user walks through how the day is divided.

PublishedMar 30, 2026 LocationTokyo Reading6 min read TagsMUJI / Store / Daily Life
01 Contents of Daily Life

Classification speaks the brand's worldview before products.

A store path is the table of contents for how a brand imagines daily life.

Contents of Daily Life

MUJI's official store categories move through clothing, cosmetics and care, daily goods, furniture with storage and appliances, and food. The list becomes a route in store and an index for reading life online.

The more precise the classification, the more a brand reveals what it considers important. When storage and appliances share a section, and scent, cleaning, and stationery sit inside daily goods, the scope of everyday life is defined.

Designing How to Walk

A good store does not pull the eye constantly. It creates a natural reason to move to the next section. MUJI's space arranges repeatable needs in sequence rather than strong events.

Taste in space comes from an order that makes people walk again more than a scene that makes them stare.

The store becomes a small city. Moving from clothes to storage and food, the visitor remeasures the scale of the life they live.
