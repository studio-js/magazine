Beauty / Haircare

Heat That Lowers Near the Scalp

The Time of Flight sensor, Scalp protect mode, Pause detect, and Attachment learning all step back in response to the user's hand rather than speaking louder. Taste in beauty technology appears through adjustment more than effect.

PublishedMay 03, 2026 LocationLondon Reading10 min read TagsDyson / Supersonic Nural / Haircare
01 A Sense of Distance

As a hair tool moves closer to the body, judgment appears as lower temperature.

Intelligence in a beauty routine is felt in temperature that lowers at the risky moment.

A Sense of Distance

Dyson describes a sensor network in Supersonic Nural that automatically activates Scalp protect mode. A Time of Flight sensor reads the distance between machine and head, lowering air temperature as it moves closer.

The feature deals with closer contact rather than faster drying alone. The user does not have to monitor heat at the scalp constantly, and the tool accepts small distance changes as part of the routine.

The closer a hair tool comes to the body, the more important it is to lower risk rather than display force. Nural's sensor feels less like surveillance that corrects mistakes and more like a way of moving the hand's repeated sense of distance into the product.

What the sensor reads is not only temperature but the distance at which a user can trust a routine. The same air should behave differently near the scalp and at the ends of hair, and when the tool reads that difference first, the hand can relax slightly.

A Machine That Knows When to Pause

Pause detect deactivates the heater and reduces airflow and noise when the dryer is put down. Attachment learning uses a Hall sensor to recognize the connected styling attachment and remember the last setting.

A good beauty tool creates repeatable assurance before perfect styling.

What makes the product interesting is that it does not ask the user to operate more. A good default in beauty technology supports repeated hand habits without exaggerating the result.

The taste of this dryer lies less in the number of new features than in how it makes the routine less anxious. A tool that quiets when set down, lowers temperature when close, and remembers attachments turns the morning into a steadier sequence.

The product finally manages the tension of the morning as much as the beauty result. Drying speed still matters, but when the user thinks less about heat, noise, and distance, technology recedes into the background of routine.
