Editor's Note

An issue that begins outside the departments

The first issue is not an index of existing categories, but a standalone editorial project. Studio surfaces, quiet interfaces, readable data, and breathable brand systems are newly arranged under one question.

This issue is closer to a sequence of edited scenes than a set of departments. It moves from an artist's desk to the defaults of a screen, while repeating one question: what turns the sense of objects into screens, and which screens return to alter judgment?

01 Cover Story How Images Remain After the Opening Essay / Editorial Desk / Seoul / 6 min An exhibition is a structure of looking that outlasts the event. We follow the time when work becomes visible again after the crowd leaves.
02 Studio Visit The Desk Is the Artist's Smallest Map Visit / Field Notes / Seongsu / 5 min Scattered tools and marked corners hide the direction of work. We read the order of the next scene from an unresolved surface.
03 Interface Report An AI Interface Without Performance Report / Systems Desk / Remote / 7 min A good tool does not keep proving its intelligence. This report asks what makes a low, useful screen that interrupts less.
04 Design Study A Brand System With Room to Breathe Study / Design Notes / Tokyo / 5 min What lasts longer than a loud logo is repeatable space. We look at a structure that lets a brand be remembered without shouting.
05 Closing Essay The Ethics of Default Settings Essay / Philosophy Desk / Seoul / 6 min A default is not a neutral setting but the strongest proposal. The closing essay returns to responsibility hidden behind the screen.
