No. 02

Rooms of Use

A second digital edition on places of use changing taste.

PublishedApril 2026 FormatOnline edition / 4 scenes AccessOpen access

Editor's Note

The editor's note for this edition

The second issue follows stores, homes, studios, and travel bags as places where objects change meaning. The same product becomes different taste depending on where it is placed.

The meaning of an object is not decided by specs alone. Reach height, room light, travel distance, and return position form taste.

The Shelf Is an Interface

Between storage and display, the shelf sets the speed of choice.

02 / Object File

The Small Room Inside a Bag

A travel bag is an object for moving and the structure of a temporary room.

A bag is a reduced room. Pockets, straps, and weather-resistant materials reduce the anxiety of movement and make temporary dwelling possible.

03 / Spatial Essay

Store Routes as Domestic Grammar

A store classifies daily life before it sells products.

Classification and route are invisible editing. In store, visitors read the brand's table of contents for life with their bodies.

04 / Afterword

Taste After Use

Taste is clearer in repetition after use than at purchase.

What one likes appears through maintenance more than purchase. Putting back, recharging, and relighting form taste.
